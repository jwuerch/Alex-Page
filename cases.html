Law Offices of
Alex J. Higgins

Labor and Employment Law

  • TRIAL AND LITIGATION EXPERIENCE (REPRESENTATIVE EXAMPLES)

    • A.C. v. Qwest (CenturyLink)
      U.S. District Court, Western District of Washington
      Prevailed at trial and obtained full relief (including punitive damages) for a judgment of over $750,000. Client was a long-term employee who experienced retaliation after raising discrimination concerns. Company was defended by a premier national law firm.
    • Brown v. AutoNation, Inc.
      King County Superior Court
      Prevailed at trial and obtained $355,000 judgment against a Bellevue car dealership for failure to make a reasonable accommodation of an employee. Company was defended by a large Seattle law firm.
    • Miles v. City of Seattle
      Public Safety Civil Service Commission
      Successfully reversed the termination of a Seattle Police Officer. Full back pay ordered.
    • Bange v. Puget Sound Electrical Workers Trust Fund and Pension Trust Fund of Local 46, International Brotherhood of Electrical Workers, AFL-CIO
      American Arbitration Association
      Obtained complete relief for individual denied early retirement benefits by union. The Union’s Pension Trust violated ERISA and the arbitrator awarded full benefits plus all attorneys’ fees and costs.
    • Coghlan v. American Seafoods, 413 F.3d 1090
      9th Cir. 2005
      Successfully represented American Seafoods in U.S. District Court and on appeal. In a published decision, the Ninth Circuit affirmed summary judgment in favor of American Seafoods on claims of national origin discrimination under Title VII and Washington law.
    • Corbin v. Anvil International
      United States District Court, Western District of Washington
      Successfully obtained complete summary judgment for Company. Judge Coughenour ordered dismissal of all claims, including claims brought under the FMLA, the Age Discrimination in Employment Act, and Washington law.
    • Crow, et. al. v. Energy Northwest
      Benton County Superior Court
      As first chair, successfully defended company from overtime claims brought by nine supervisory employees. Two-week jury trial on all nine plaintiffs’ claims. Complete defense verdict, finding that the nine supervisors were properly classified as exempt.
    • Lively v. Sea-Land Service, Inc.,
      United States District Court, Western District of Washington
      Obtained complete summary judgment for the employer on a seaman’s claims of age discrimination and wrongful termination in violation of public policy.
    • Western Wireless Corporation v. Omniserve Wireless and William H. Curtis
      United States District Court, Western District of Washington
      Successfully obtained a preliminary injunction based on a non-competition agreement.
    • Shaw v. Energy Northwest
      United States District Court, Eastern District of Washington
      Obtained verdict for client after a 6-day jury trial. Plaintiff was a long-term African-American employee who claimed race discrimination and retaliation. The jury rendered a complete defense verdict.
